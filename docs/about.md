## My mathematical path

I studied mathematics as an undergraduate in China and later completed a master’s degree at **Shanghai Jiao Tong University**, where I worked in **combinatorial commutative algebra**.

As I kept learning, my interests expanded toward algebraic and analytic number theory, automorphic forms, representation theory, probability, and homogeneous dynamics. I am fascinated by mathematics that reveals hidden structure: primes, spectra, symmetries, orbits, and the unexpected bridges between different theories.

  </div>

  <div class="about-card" markdown="1">

## What I am exploring now

The main themes of this website are:

- analytic and algebraic number theory,
- automorphic forms and spectral ideas,
- representation theory,
- homogeneous dynamics,
- probability in arithmetic,
- and papers or seminar talks I want to understand more deeply.

## Why this homepage exists

I wanted one place where I could:

- keep my notes organized,
- remember what I have learned,
- make difficult ideas a little clearer,
- and leave room for questions that are still unfinished.

This site is not meant to be perfect. It is meant to be **alive** — a record of learning in progress.
